Subordinated Loans in Broker-Dealer Operations

Understanding structure, requirements, and implications for net capital

Back to Main

Basic Requirements

  • Minimum term of 1 year
  • Must be in writing
  • Lender must subordinate claims to other creditors
  • Explicit acknowledgment of risk in operations

Types of Subordinated Agreements

Cash Subordinated Loan

Direct cash provided to broker-dealer

Impact: Direct increase in firm's available capital

Secured Demand Note

Collateralized by marketable securities

Securities pledged to bank with specific valuation rules

Collateral Requirements

Equity Securities

  • Bank lending value: 70% of market value
  • 30% haircut on secured demand note collateral

Other Securities

  • Standard net capital haircuts apply
  • Additional haircuts for concentration

Important Considerations

Risk Factors

  • Concentration limits on collateral
  • Mark-to-market requirements
  • Regular valuation of collateral
