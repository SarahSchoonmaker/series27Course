What is the Net Capital Rule?

The Net Capital Rule (SEC Rule 15c3-1) is a financial responsibility rule that requires broker-dealers to maintain minimum levels of liquid assets against their liabilities to protect customers and creditors.

Core Objectives

  • Protect customer assets
  • Ensure broker-dealer financial stability
  • Maintain market integrity
  • Provide early warning of financial difficulties

Key Requirements

  • Maintain minimum liquid capital
  • Calculate net capital accurately
  • Monitor financial health continuously
  • Report deficiencies promptly

Calculation Frequency Requirements

Broker-Dealer Type Minimum Requirement Calculation Frequency Filing Requirements
Clearing Broker-Dealers $250,000 Greater of:
  • $250,000
  • 2% of aggregate debit items
  • 6⅔% of aggregate indebtedness
  • Daily net capital computation
  • Continuous monitoring
  • Real-time position tracking
  • Monthly FOCUS filing
  • Quarterly Financial Reports
  • Annual Audited Statements
Introducing Broker-Dealers $50,000 Greater of:
  • $50,000
  • 6⅔% of aggregate indebtedness
  • Monthly computation required
  • More frequent if near minimum
  • Weekly if under early warning
  • Quarterly FOCUS filing
  • Annual Audited Statements
Limited Purpose Broker-Dealers $5,000 Greater of:
  • $5,000
  • 6⅔% of aggregate indebtedness
  • Monthly computation required
  • Quarterly if consistently above minimums
  • Quarterly FOCUS filing
  • Annual Audited Statements

Additional Monitoring Requirements

Early Warning Requirements

Must notify SEC if net capital falls below 120% of required minimum

Triggers more frequent calculations and reporting
Special Circumstances

More frequent calculations required during:

  • Market volatility
  • Significant trading losses
  • Material operational changes
  • Customer complaint increases
Technology Requirements

Firms must maintain systems capable of:

  • Real-time position monitoring
  • Automated net capital calculations
  • Early warning threshold alerts
  • Regulatory reporting generation
