Net Capital Computation Process

The net capital computation follows a systematic process to determine a broker-dealer's liquid capital available to meet obligations.

Step 1: Calculate Net Worth

Begin with the basic accounting equation:

Net Worth = Total Assets - Total Liabilities
Represents the broker-dealer's basic financial position before regulatory adjustments.
↓

Step 2: Add Subordinated Loans

Add qualifying subordinated loans to net worth:

Total Available Capital = Net Worth + Subordinated Loans
Subordinated loans must have formal agreements where lenders subordinate their claims to other creditors. These loans enhance the firm's capital base for regulatory purposes.
↓

Step 3: Deduct Non-allowable Assets

Remove assets not readily convertible to cash:

Tentative Net Capital = Total Available Capital - Non-allowable Assets
Non-allowable assets typically include:
  • Fixed assets
  • Prepaid expenses
  • Unsecured receivables
  • Intangible assets
↓

Step 4: Apply Haircuts

Apply percentage deductions to allowable securities:

Final Net Capital = Tentative Net Capital - Securities Haircuts
Common haircut percentages:
  • Equity securities: 15%
  • Corporate debt: 2-9%
  • U.S. government securities: 0-6%
  • Municipal securities: 2-7%

Final Net Capital

The resulting amount represents the broker-dealer's liquid capital available to meet obligations and regulatory requirements.

This final figure must meet minimum requirements based on either:
  • The basic method (6⅔% of aggregate indebtedness)
  • The alternative method ($250,000 or 2% of aggregate debit items)
