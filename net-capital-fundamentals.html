Understanding Net Capital Requirements

Back to Main
1What is Net Capital?

Net Capital is the broker-dealer's liquid assets minus all liabilities and certain adjustments that reflect potential market and credit risks. It is a measure designed to ensure that broker-dealers maintain sufficient liquid assets to protect customer funds and meet obligations to creditors.

Key Components:
  • Liquid assets available to meet obligations
  • Deductions for non-allowable assets
  • Haircuts on securities positions
  • Adjustments for market and credit risks
2Why is Net Capital Calculated?

Customer Protection

  • Ensures sufficient funds for customer claims
  • Protects customer assets during firm stress
  • Maintains market stability

Regulatory Compliance

  • Meets SEC Rule 15c3-1 requirements
  • Demonstrates financial responsibility
  • Prevents operational risks

Risk Management

  • Monitors financial health
  • Identifies potential issues early
  • Maintains business continuity
3Calculation Frequency Requirements
Clearing Broker-Dealers Daily
  • Daily net capital computation
  • Real-time position monitoring
  • Monthly FOCUS filing
  • Quarterly financial reports
  • Annual audited statements
Required for firms with $250,000 minimum net capital
Introducing Broker-Dealers Monthly
  • Monthly computation required
  • Weekly if under early warning
  • Quarterly FOCUS filing
  • Annual audited statements
Required for firms with $50,000 minimum net capital
Limited Purpose Broker-Dealers Quarterly
  • Monthly computation standard
  • Quarterly if consistently compliant
  • Quarterly FOCUS filing
  • Annual audited statements
Required for firms with $5,000 minimum net capital
4Special Considerations

Early Warning Requirements

  • Increased monitoring when net capital falls below 120% of required minimum
  • Immediate notification to regulators required
  • More frequent calculations may be required

Additional Monitoring Requirements

  • During periods of market volatility
  • When experiencing significant trading losses
  • During material operational changes
  • When customer complaints increase
