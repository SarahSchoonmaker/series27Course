Broker-Dealer Types and Net Capital Requirements

Broker-Dealer Type Core Activities Requirements & Permissions
Clearing Broker-Dealer
$250,000 Minimum
Greater of: • $250,000 • 2% of aggregate debit items
  • Carries customer accounts
  • Accepts customer funds and securities
  • Sends trade confirmations
  • Issues customer statements
  • Maintains customer records
Permitted Activities:
  • Full underwriting participation
  • Can act as syndicate member
  • May collect customer funds
  • Unlimited trading activities
Fully Disclosed Broker-Dealer
$50,000 Standard
Increases to $100,000 for: • >10 proprietary trades • Writing OTC options
  • Does not hold customer funds
  • Uses clearing firm agreement
  • Processes trades through clearing firm
  • Takes customer orders
  • Provides investment advice
Permitted:
  • Accept mutual fund orders
  • Best efforts underwriting
  • Limited proprietary trading
Restrictions:
  • No customer fund holding
  • No firm commitment underwriting
Limited Purpose Broker-Dealer
$5,000 Minimum
Fixed requirement for specific activities
  • Limited partnerships
  • M&A advisory services
  • Private placements
  • No customer funds/securities
Permitted:
  • Best efforts syndicate participation
  • M&A advisory activities
Restrictions:
  • No securities transactions
  • No customer fund handling
  • No firm commitment underwriting
