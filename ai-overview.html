Aggregate Indebtedness (AI)

Understanding the core metrics of broker-dealer debt obligations

What is Aggregate Indebtedness?

Aggregate Indebtedness represents all unsecured liabilities and customer-related liabilities of a broker-dealer that would need to be paid in liquidation.

First Year Operations

Maximum AI/NC Ratio

8:1

During the first year of operations

After First Year

Maximum AI/NC Ratio

15:1

Standard ongoing ratio requirement

Practical Example

For a firm with $400,000 Net Capital:

Maximum AI = 15 × Net Capital
Maximum AI = 15 × $400,000 = $6,000,000

The firm cannot have aggregate indebtedness exceeding $6,000,000

Components of Aggregate Indebtedness

  • All unsecured broker-dealer liabilities
  • Customer-related liabilities
  • Accounts payable and accrued expenses
  • Non-customer related payables

Key Considerations

  • Clearing firms must maintain minimum $250,000 regardless of ratio
  • Firms must increase net capital as debt levels rise
  • Subordinated loans do not count in AI calculation
  • Regular monitoring and reporting required

Minimum Net Capital Example

For a clearing firm with $300,000 AI:

Minimum NC = AI ÷ 15
Minimum NC = $300,000 ÷ 15 = $20,000
However: Clearing firm minimum = $250,000
Therefore: Required NC = $250,000
